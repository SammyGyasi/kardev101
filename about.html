Our Ambitions

AfricaMed Business Forum is a premium event aimed at promoting Mediterranean and international investment in Africa and economic development of Africa in the Mediterranean region and the world. It is designed as a high-level forum to bring together influential public and private figures, heads of state, ministers, and political officials from Africa and the Mediterranean, representatives of continental organizations responsible for economic development, private sector companies, and other regional actors. The Forum is a platform for dialogue and exchange for entrepreneurs from Africa and the Mediterranean, where they can explore business opportunities, exchange views, share experiences, and discuss the challenges they face. With nearly 500 high-level participants, the Forum aims to foster strong public-private partnerships to drive economic development and strengthen trade relations between Africa and the Mediterranean region. AfricaMed Business Forum also offers a unique opportunity to meet experts in different sectors such as agriculture, energy, infrastructure, information and communication technologies, and participate in workshops and training sessions to improve their economic development skills. In summary, AfricaMed Business Forum is a must-attend event for entrepreneurs, policymakers, investors, and private sector actors interested in economic development in Africa and the Mediterranean region. The Forum offers a unique platform to foster strong public-private partnerships, explore business opportunities, and discuss the challenges facing entrepreneurs in this rapidly changing region of the world.

Our Objectives

We believe that greater investment and stronger industrialization are needed to enable sustainable and inclusive growth, the creation of new jobs, and the generation of wealth in Africa and throughout the Mediterranean region. AfricaMed Business Forum will promote open and in-depth dialogue between public decision-makers, private partners and investors, both foreign and local, to develop and promote investment and trade in the region.

  1. Bring together high-level public and private figures, including heads of state, ministers, and political officials from Africa and the Mediterranean, representatives of continental organizations responsible for economic development, private sector companies, and other regional actors.
  2. Provide an exchange platform for entrepreneurs in Africa and the Mediterranean to share best practices, explore opportunities for economic growth, and promote value chain development.
  3. Encourage strong public-private partnerships to drive economic development and strengthen trade relations between Africa and the Mediterranean, with a focus on developing the value chain and increasing exports of African products.
